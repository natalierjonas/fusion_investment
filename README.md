# **Billions flow into fusion. But, no reactors are producing power.**

### The Trump administration (and venture capital) is pouring hundreds of millions of dollars into nuclear energy companies — oftentimes the same few — hoping to spur on an energy revolution.


### **GOALS**
#### Nuclear fusion tech hasn't yet worked but billions of dollars have been invested. Venture capital investment is largely characterized by throwing darts at a board, but I was especially interested in how fusion tech is being treated by those in power, with money: venture capitalists, Big Tech and the government. Conflicts of interest abound in this field, especially due to Trump's recent $6 billion merger with Tae Technologies. The obvious fallacy in fusion investment — funneling *so much money* — into the not-yet-viable, interested me greatly. And, the industry shows no signs of slowing down.

### **SOME FINDINGS**
#### Trump and others in his administration are engaging in clear COIs, particularly within the newly formed Office of Fusion. Some companies have received funding from all three of the main grant programs and the homogoneous grant-giving portfolio certainly needs a closer look. 

#### Chris Wright's "revolving door" politics, Sam Altman's investment in the space and the Trump merger with Tae — likely to be the first publicly traded fusion company — will soon be much bigger news and I look forward to investigating this more!
